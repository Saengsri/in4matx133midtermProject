Summary of Findings

My goal for the user research was to discover what users, specifically graduating college students, were looking for as they were graduating and how were the attempting to standout. Based off my research, students wanted to have an easy solution to standing-out-amongst-the-crowd problem. Also, when looking at competitors, I wanted to see how other places, like LinkedIn and Gary Vaynerchuk, were displaying online portfolios of an individual. In terms of facing any challenges during this point, I didn't face any.

In terms of user testing, I used the cognitive walkthroughs to focus more qualitative information and for the user testing, I focused more on the quantitive information. Overall I personally used the user testing to validate if the users approved of my simplistic design and personallity I added to my portfolio. For the most part, they did with a little individual changes here and there.

Incorporation of Findings

I applied stylistic changes from what I gathered from the user testing if I agreed with the responses. There were nuisances in my findings that I felt that were necessarily applicable because it might have applied to a small representation of the population. Some of the stylistic changes were making texts appear more friendly to users, by their color and size. Also, from the time I submitted the midterm project and until now, my text for whatever reason shifted, so I moved it to a more appropriate spot.

If I had more time, some the changes I wanted to apply to my website would be improving the responsive design, such as making it better for the mobile version. With my current level of skills and time left, I couldn't make it better for the mobile version. One way to make the mobile version is make the website adaptive to the user's device. Also, another small thing a user pointed out was that he was color-blind. Some of the color schemes on the personal page did not favor him well.
